CLTR

Chrono-Light Thermal Response is a collaborative research initiative that dissects the interplay between physiological adaptation, perceptual response, and behavioral regulation during warm indoor exposure, specifically isolating the effects of controlled variations in illuminance, diurnal timing, and airflow.

Work Package

CLTR serves as the shared project framework for the EU Horizon MuSIC initiative, encompassing IRP1 and IRP2 research streams. It connects experimental evidence, multimodal datasets, and machine learning models with analytical outputs in ways that advance scientific understanding and inform future domain practice.

What It Brings

A unified project structure linking physiology, indoor environmental measurements, perception, and analytical workflow in one coherent research platform.

What It Aims

To produce robust datasets, interpretable evidence, and reusable outputs for indoor environmental science, thermal comfort research, and intelligent control.

Who Is Involved

CLTR brings together Abdelkader Elkounni, Puneet Tomar, Marika Vellei, Jérôme Le Dréau, Alfonso P. Ramallo-González, Anna Laura Pisello, and Christian Inard through the MuSIC collaboration.

Atlas Experiment

Collaboration

A joint CLTR study under MUSIC

CLTR connects the work of two doctoral tracks within the MUSIC project, but it stands as its own integrated study. The project is structured so steady-state and acute dynamic responses can be interpreted together rather than as separate experiments.

Description

Controlled perception and response mapping

The study combines continuous physiological signals, environmental measurements, repeated thermal ratings, and structured fan-cooling and rewarming phases to produce a richer map of indoor human response for thermal comfort, chrono-physiology, and intelligent building control research.
